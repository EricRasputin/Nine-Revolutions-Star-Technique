# Chapter 5: The Wang Family Rises!

Three months later.

Yebei City, inside the Wang Manor!

The entire estate was hung with lanterns and festive banners, as though celebrating some great occasion.

At the manor gates, a steady stream of visitors had arrived bearing gifts.

Out on the street, a good number of ordinary townspeople had gathered in front of the gates, pointing and muttering among themselves.

"Tsk. The Wang Manor is about to make quite a name for itself."

"You can say that again. Word is their young miss stepped into the Divine Martial Realm just yesterday, making her the fastest prodigy ever to reach the Divine Martial Realm in all of Yebei City's history. The whole city is buzzing. Even the Great Xia Dynasty, hundreds of miles away, sent the Third Prince himself to offer congratulations."

"And it's not just the Great Xia Dynasty. Moongazing Sect, one of the top-tier sects in the region, has also dispatched a Recruitment Elder hoping to take Wang Yiyi in."

"Yebei City is about to fall completely under the Wang Family's thumb."

"Who's arguing? The young miss of the City Lord's Manor is still sitting at the eighth grade of the Tri-Profound Realm, still a fair way from the Divine Martial Realm. As for the Su Family... sigh. If that prodigy Su Yang had never disappeared, I doubt the Wang Family would be holding their heads this high today."

"Shh, lower your voice. You don't want anyone from the manor to hear you. Watch your tongue."

"...."

"Yah~!"

"Yah~!"

"Look! Someone from the Su Family is here."

"That's right. That's the Su Family's Grand Elder, their current patriarch — Su Beilou."

"Who knows where the two families stand these days. They were bound by a marriage arrangement once, after all. But the way things look now, the Wang Family seems ready to disown that connection entirely."

"....."

"A warm welcome to the Su Family Patriarch. Our patriarch and the City Lord have long been awaiting your arrival." A servant at the gate stepped forward with a ready smile.

Su Beilou's expression, however, was anything but warm.

He had spent decades scraping and clawing his way through Yebei City. Of course he could see that after today, the gap between the Su Family and the Wang Family would only grow — that one day the Su Family might cease to exist altogether.

"Hmph!" Su Beilou let out a cold snort and, flanked by several of the Su Family's top fighters, strode inside.

The servant took no offense. He kept his smile in place, ready to greet the next round of guests arriving with gifts.

....

Inside the Wang Manor's main hall!

The moment Su Beilou stepped through the doors, a hearty laugh rang out: "Ha ha ha! Brother Beilou, forgive me for not coming to receive you in person."

Wang Lang sat comfortably in his grand armchair, face flushed with satisfaction as he watched Su Beilou enter, not the least bit inclined to rise.

Su Beilou swept the room with a single glance, and his expression shifted.

Every person seated in the Wang Manor's hall was, without exception, a figure of enormous standing.

The man sitting on equal footing with Wang Lang was none other than the Third Prince of the Great Xia Dynasty himself, and at Wang Lang's right hand sat the City Lord, Li Beiye.

What chilled Su Beilou even more was the figure seated across from Li Beiye — an elderly woman with long black hair, her face deeply lined with wrinkles, yet radiating an aura of crushing authority.

The characters embroidered on her robes read: Moongazing.

Su Beilou knew exactly what that name meant. Moongazing Sect was a premier power, a force that stood as a peer to the Great Xia Dynasty itself, its sect master's ties to the dynasty running deep and tangled. The sheer depth of its strength was almost beyond imagining.

Even a force of that caliber had come personally to pay its respects to the Wang Family. It seemed nothing could stop the Wang Family's rise now.

Faced with this reality, Su Beilou understood he needed to know his place. He clasped his hands and bowed: "Patriarch Wang, there's really no need for ceremony. You're a busy man today — it's only natural you couldn't attend to me."

"Ha ha! Please, sit down, sit down!" Wang Lang laughed again.

"Patriarch Wang, so this is the head of the Su Family you were telling me about?" the Third Prince, seated beside Wang Lang, asked.

"Yes, Your Highness. This is the Su Family Patriarch, Su Beilou."

"Never heard of him. Though I recall the Su Family had quite a gifted young man three years ago. Is this the same Su Family?" The Third Prince studied Su Beilou with a steady gaze.

"Indeed it is, Your Highness. But that was three years ago. My daughter even had a betrothal arrangement with the Su Family's young master at the time. Unfortunately... he vanished from Yebei City. Perhaps he felt too ashamed — afraid that his condition might tarnish the Su Family's reputation — and so he simply... went away." Wang Lang added by way of explanation.

At that, everyone present couldn't help but perk up.

Su Beilou himself was caught off guard. All these years, he had served as Su Family Patriarch — but only because the Wang Family had been propping him up from the shadows. It was Wang Lang who had sought him out three years ago, offering to install him as patriarch and dangling terms he could not refuse.

Su Beilou had never been in a position to refuse. Yet all these years, one question had gnawed at him: why had Su Yang, the Su Family's young master, a young man bearing the Supreme Bloodline, vanished so quietly?

He had long suspected the Wang Family's hand in it. But Su Beilou had always told himself: as long as he got to be patriarch, what did Su Yang's fate have to do with him? Now that Wang Lang seemed ready to speak the truth of that disappearance, Su Beilou found he very much wanted to hear it.

"Oh? What exactly do you mean by that, Patriarch Wang? Was the young master hiding something?" the Third Prince asked, his curiosity plain.

Just as Wang Lang was about to explain, a low, measured voice drifted into the hall: "If I may, Your Highness. Su Yang and I were engaged, and he did wish to share my chambers. But it turned out... he was incapable of it. He was likely afraid the news would spread and bring shame upon the Su Family. So he..."

The words trailed off. Wang Yiyi stepped into the hall.

A deep blue gown wrapped her graceful figure, and her lightly made-up face wore an expression of shy embarrassment, as though speaking those words had taken every last shred of her courage. But her eyes were as cold as ever.

The entire hall fell quiet.

Only Su Beilou stood there looking ill, his fists clenched, as if unable to bring himself to accept what he had heard.

The Third Prince cleared his throat awkwardly: "Ahem. Is that so. A minor ailment, nothing more. We have no shortage of master healers in the Great Xia Dynasty. Was there really need to go off in such a huff?"

"Ha, His Highness is quite right. But Su Yang was quite the celebrated figure in Yebei City back in his day — young and full of hot blood. I suppose it's understandable. Ah, who knows where my former son-in-law is now, and whether that hidden ailment of his has improved at all..." Wang Lang offered a small laugh, then let a look of deep sorrow settle over his face.

At that, Su Beilou finally snapped: "Patriarch Wang, do you really need to be so blunt about it? And you just said 'former son-in-law' — does that mean he no longer is one?"

"Patriarch Su, are you joking? Do you think Wang Yiyi would ever marry a useless man? Much less one whose fate is entirely unknown."

"You really do underestimate me, Patriarch Su."

"The reason I summoned you here today was precisely to make one thing clear: from this day forward, Wang Yiyi wants nothing to do with the Su Family."

"And I intend to... break off my engagement to Su Yang!!"
