# Chapter 3: The Great Sage in the Stone

"Great Sage, say no more. Those three years — I will never forget them as long as I live! If you have any way to restore the Supreme Blood in my body and let me take my revenge, I will gladly serve you like a beast of burden for the rest of my days!"

Su Yang said through gritted teeth.

"Heh heh, you can forget the beast of burden business. With your level of cultivation, this Great Sage wouldn't even bother. But if it weren't for the Supreme Blood in your body, this Great Sage wouldn't have awakened so quickly. When it comes down to it, I actually owe you some thanks."

"In a moment, I will restore the vitality in your body. When that's done, find somewhere with no people and lay low."

The words had barely faded when Su Yang felt a familiar surge of heat flooding into his body, coursing through his Eight Extraordinary Meridians and spreading to every limb and bone. His skeletal frame began to grow flesh and muscle once more, his vitality steadily returning...

Like a spring bamboo shoot pushing through the earth, life awakened within him — surging, ceaseless.

Before long, Su Yang was able to stand. He swam out of the pool and crawled ashore, kneeling on the ground, staring at his hands — hands full of flesh and blood once more. Excitement blazed in his eyes.

"I... my flesh — it's back!"

"Ha ha ha ha! My body is restored! My Supreme Blood has not run dry!"

Su Yang's eyes reddened, and two lines of hot tears rolled slowly down his cheeks.

He wanted to scream, to release the inhuman torment he had endured all those years.

But he held it in. He knew that he had only regained his flesh — there was not a shred of spiritual energy in his body. If he cried out now and attracted a demonic beast, he would end up as nothing more than a monster's meal and a pile of dung.

"Don't celebrate too soon, kid. There's a powerful demonic beast somewhere nearby, and it's not far from you. If you'd rather not die a second time, find somewhere to hide — now." The Great Sage's voice echoed through his mind once again.

Su Yang scrambled to his feet, looking left and right, and spotted a small stone cave not far from the pool.

The cave was not large — just big enough for Su Yang to squeeze inside.

He ducked in, but not three seconds later he came tearing back out like a gust of wind — with a snarling wild wolf hot on his heels.

"Shit! Damned beast!" Su Yang cursed.

"...You carry the Supreme Bloodline, for crying out loud. You may not have any spiritual energy right now, but surely you can handle a single wild animal?" The exasperated voice rang through Su Yang's mind once more.

Su Yang skidded to a halt, blinking in realization. "Oh, right. I've got my body back — why would I be afraid of one measly beast?"

"Damn it!"

Su Yang felt a stab of shame at his own cowardice. Three years caged like livestock had made him forget the thrill of battle!

"Awoooo!" The wolf bared its fangs, glinting green eyes locked on Su Yang.

"Hmph! You nearly scared me off, you mutt. Perfect — it's been too long since I've had the taste of a fight. Today, you'll help me find that feeling again!" Su Yang clenched both fists, and a strength he had not felt in ages surged through his entire body.

"Haaah!"

With a roar, his body blurred forward. His right fist shot out and hammered down toward the wolf's skull.

A seemingly ordinary punch, yet it split the air with a sharp crack. The wolf seemed to sense that the human before it was no ordinary prey and tried to turn and flee — only to find its body locked in place. It managed a single, pitiful howl before Su Yang's fist blasted it into the rock face above the cave, and it slid slowly to the ground.

Its skull cracked open like a watermelon... red and white matter oozing together in a slow trickle.

"Not bad at all. Just got your flesh back and you already pack that kind of punch."

The voice echoed through his mind again.

Su Yang glanced at the thoroughly dead wolf, savoring the rush of power that had just exploded through him, and allowed himself a smile he had not worn in a very long time. "That feeling... damn, it feels good."

Then his gaze turned toward some distant, unseen point, and killing intent hardened his features. "Wang Family. Wang Yiyi. Your good days are numbered."

...

Meanwhile, in Yebei City, inside the Wang Manor.

"Patriarch Wang, the job is done — we brothers have taken care of everything."

"After all these years, it's time you honored the promise you made us." Three black-clad figures stared at Wang Lang, who sat enthroned before them.

"Ha ha, but of course. You three have done no small amount of work for this patriarch over the years, and I naturally intend to fulfill my promises. However... there is one matter I forgot to mention." Wang Lang lifted the teacup beside him, holding the lid with his right hand.

"Oh? And what might that be, Patriarch Wang?" The leader of the three frowned.

"Heh heh, it's nothing, really. I simply wanted to let you three know — those who seek to take something from Wang Lang... well, I may give, but you won't live to collect!" The instant the words left his mouth, a surge of devastating power erupted from Wang Lang's body.

The three men in black knew exactly what was coming.

Their expressions shifted in unison. Without a word, the leader unleashed his own burst of power and launched a barrage of fists at Wang Lang: "You treacherous bastard, Wang Lang! You think you can stab us in the back? Did you really believe we came unprepared? If the three of us don't walk out of this manor alive today, within the hour, every dirty deed the Wang Family has ever done will be spread across all of Yebei City. The City Lord's Manor will not let you off lightly."

Wang Lang did not so much as flinch at the assault, merely offering a faint smile. "Oh? So you suspected I would silence you all along? Clever. But even if word gets out — what of it?"

"Once my daughter steps into the Divine Martial Realm, what can a mere City Lord's Manor possibly do to the Wang Family?"

"You three know far too much. Best you hurry along to your next life — and try not to be such fools next time around."

"Die!"

BOOM—

A blast of spiritual energy erupted and instantly split into three devastating waves, each one hurtling toward a fleeing figure. The terrifying force gave the men in black no chance to resist.

Moments later, three corpses tumbled from the air...

Utterly lifeless.

"You three served the Wang Family faithfully, I'll grant you that. But in the end, you were only outsiders."

"Guards — drag these bodies out and feed them to the dogs."

Wang Lang set down his teacup, his tone cold as ice — the voice of a devil.

Deep within the Wang Manor, a slender, beautiful figure sat in closed-eye cultivation. Two distinct bloodline auras surged within her body, ceaselessly merging, ceaselessly roiling...

The aura emanating from her was climbing at a staggering pace — higher and higher, with no sign of stopping!
