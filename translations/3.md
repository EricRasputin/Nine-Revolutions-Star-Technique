# Chapter 3: The Great Sage in the Stone

"Great Sage, say no more. Those three years — I will never forget them as long as I live! If you have any way to restore the Supreme Blood in my body and let me avenge this grudge, I will gladly serve you like an ox and a horse for the rest of my days!"

Su Yang said through gritted teeth.

"Heh heh, forget the ox and horse business. With your level of cultivation, this Great Sage couldn't be bothered. Still, if it weren't for the Supreme Blood in your body, this Great Sage wouldn't have awakened so quickly — so I suppose I owe you some thanks."

"In a moment, I'll restore the vitality in your body. When that's done, find somewhere with no people and lay low."

The words had barely faded when Su Yang felt a familiar surge of warmth flooding into his body, coursing through the Eight Extraordinary Meridians and spreading to every limb and bone. His skeletal frame began to sprout flesh and muscle once more, his vitality steadily returning...

Like a spring bamboo shoot pushing through the earth, life stirred within him — surging, ceaseless.

Before long, Su Yang was able to stand — he swam out of the pool and crawled ashore, kneeling on the ground, staring at his hands, hands full of flesh and blood once more, excitement blazing in his eyes.

"I... my flesh — it's back!"

"Ha ha ha ha! My body is restored — my Supreme Blood hasn't run dry!"

Su Yang's eyes reddened, and two lines of hot tears rolled slowly down his cheeks.

He wanted to scream, to release the inhuman torment he had endured all those years.

But he held it in — he knew he had only regained his flesh, with not a shred of spiritual energy in his body. If he cried out now and attracted a demonic beast, he would truly end up as a monster's meal and a pile of dung.

"Don't celebrate too soon, kid. There's a powerful demonic beast nearby, and it's not far from you — if you'd rather not die a second time, find somewhere to hide, and do it now." The Great Sage's voice echoed through his mind once more.

Su Yang scrambled to his feet, glancing left and right, and spotted a small stone cave not far from the pool.

The cave was small — just large enough for Su Yang to squeeze inside.

He ducked in, but not three seconds later he came tearing back out like the wind — with a snarling wild wolf hot on his heels.

"Shit! Damned beast!" Su Yang cursed.

"...You carry the Supreme Bloodline, for crying out loud. You may not have any spiritual energy right now, but surely you can handle one wild animal?" The exasperated voice rang through Su Yang's mind.

Su Yang skidded to a halt, blinking. "Oh, right. I've got my body back — why would I be afraid of one measly wolf?"

"Damn it!"

Su Yang felt a stab of shame at his own cowardice — three years caged like livestock had made him forget the thrill of battle!

"Awoooo!" The wolf bared its fangs, glinting green eyes locked on Su Yang.

"Hmph! You nearly scared me off, you mutt. Perfect timing — it's been far too long since I've tasted a real fight. Today, you'll help me get that feeling back!" Su Yang clenched both fists as a strength he had not felt in ages surged through his body.

"Haaah!"

With a roar, his body blurred forward — his right fist shot out and hammered down toward the wolf's skull.

A seemingly ordinary punch, yet it split the air with a sharp crack. The wolf sensed that the human before it was no ordinary prey and tried to flee — only to find its body locked in place. A single, pitiful howl escaped its throat before Su Yang's fist blasted it into the rock face above the cave, and it slid slowly to the ground.

Its skull cracked apart like a watermelon... red and white matter oozing together in a slow trickle.

"Not bad — just got your flesh back and you already pack that kind of punch."

The voice echoed through his mind again.

Su Yang glanced at the thoroughly dead wolf, savoring the rush of power that had just exploded through him, and allowed himself a smile he had not worn in a very long time. "That feeling... damn, it feels good."

Then his gaze fixed on some distant, unseen point, and killing intent hardened his features. "Wang Family. Wang Yiyi. Your good days are numbered."

...

Meanwhile, in Yebei City — inside the Wang Manor.

"Patriarch Wang, the job is done — we brothers have taken care of everything."

"After all these years, it's time you honored the promise you made us." The three black-clad figures stood before Wang Lang, who sat enthroned above them.

"Ha ha, but of course. You three have done no small amount of work for this patriarch over the years, and I naturally intend to honor my word. However... there is one thing I forgot to mention." Wang Lang lifted the teacup beside him, holding the lid with his right hand.

"Oh? What might that be, Patriarch Wang?" The leader of the three frowned.

"Heh heh, it's nothing, really. I simply wanted you three to know — when it comes to taking something from Wang Lang, I may be willing to give... but you won't live to collect." The instant the words left his mouth, a devastating surge of power erupted from Wang Lang's body.

The three men in black knew exactly what was coming.

Their expressions shifted in unison — without a word, the leader unleashed his own burst of power and hurled a barrage of fists at Wang Lang: "Wang Lang, you treacherous bastard! You think we came unprepared? If the three of us don't walk out of this manor alive today, within the hour every dirty deed your Wang Family has done will be spread across all of Yebei City. The City Lord's Manor will not let you off!"

Wang Lang did not so much as flinch at the assault, merely offering a thin smile: "Oh? So you've suspected I would silence you all along? Impressive. But even if the word gets out — what of it?"

"Once my daughter steps into the Divine Martial Realm, what can a mere City Lord's Manor possibly do to the Wang Family?"

"You three know far too much. Best you hurry along to your next lives — and try not to be such fools next time."

"Die!"

BOOM—

A blast of spiritual energy erupted and instantly split into three devastating waves, each hurtling toward one of the fleeing figures — the terrifying power gave the men in black no chance to resist.

In mere moments, three corpses tumbled from the air...

Utterly lifeless.

"You three served the Wang Family faithfully, I'll grant you that. But in the end, you were only outsiders."

"Guards — drag these bodies out and feed them to the dogs."

Wang Lang set down his teacup, his tone cold as ice — like a devil incarnate.

Deep within the Wang Manor, a slender, beautiful figure sat in closed-eye cultivation, two distinct bloodline auras surging within her body — ceaselessly merging, ceaselessly roiling...

The aura emanating from her was climbing at a staggering pace — higher and higher, with no sign of stopping!
