# Chapter 3: The Great Sage in the Stone

"Great Sage, say no more. Those three years — I will never forget them as long as I live. If you have any way to restore my Supreme Blood and let me avenge this debt, I will gladly serve you like an ox, like a horse, for the rest of my days."

Su Yang ground the words out through clenched teeth.

"Heh heh. Forget the ox and horse. With your cultivation base, this Great Sage couldn't be bothered. Still, if it weren't for the Supreme Blood in your body, I wouldn't have awakened so quickly. So I suppose I owe you some thanks as well."

"In a moment, I'll restore the vitality in your body. When that's done, find somewhere with no people and lay low."

The words had barely faded when Su Yang felt a familiar surge of warmth flood into him, rushing through his Eight Extraordinary Meridians and spreading to every limb and bone. The skeletal frame he had become began to grow flesh again, vitality returning by steady degrees.

Like a spring shoot forcing its way through cold earth, life stirred inside him, steady and relentless.

Before long, Su Yang could stand. He dragged himself out of the pool and crawled ashore, kneeling on the ground, staring at his hands — hands with flesh on them again — and felt excitement ignite in his eyes.

"I... my body. It's back!"

"Ha ha ha ha! I'm restored. My Supreme Blood hasn't run dry!"

Su Yang's eyes reddened, and two lines of hot tears slid slowly down his face.

He wanted to scream. To release the years of inhuman suffering all at once.

But he held it in. He had flesh back, but not a shred of spiritual energy. If he made noise and drew a demonic beast now, he would truly end up as a monster's meal.

"Don't celebrate too soon, kid. There's a powerful demonic beast nearby, and it isn't far. If you'd rather not die a second time, find somewhere to hide and do it now." The Great Sage's voice cut through his mind.

Su Yang scrambled to his feet, looked around, and spotted a small stone cave not far from the pool.

Small enough to fit only him, but it would do.

He ducked inside — and not three seconds later came tearing back out like a gust of wind, a snarling wild wolf hot on his heels.

"Damn it! Filthy beast!" Su Yang cursed.

"...You carry the Supreme Bloodline, for crying out loud. You may have no spiritual energy right now, but surely you can handle one wild wolf?" The exasperated voice rang through his head.

Su Yang skidded to a halt, blinking. "Oh, right. I've got my body back. Why would I run from a wolf?"

"Damn it!"

A flash of shame hit him. Three years caged like livestock had stripped the fighting instinct right out of him.

"Awoooo!" The wolf bared its fangs, green eyes locked on Su Yang with cold hunger.

"Hmph! Nearly scared me off, you mutt. Perfect timing. It's been far too long since I've had a real fight. Today you'll help me remember what that feels like." Su Yang clenched both fists, and a strength he had not felt in years surged through his body.

"Haah!"

He roared and blurred forward, right fist driving toward the wolf's skull.

A simple punch, but it split the air with a sharp crack. The wolf sensed the human before it was no ordinary prey and tried to bolt, only to find every muscle locked in place. A single howl tore from its throat before Su Yang's fist blasted it into the rock face above the cave entrance, and it slid down and hit the ground.

Its skull had cracked open like a watermelon, red and white spilling out together in a slow, spreading mess.

"Not bad. You just got your flesh back and you're already hitting like that."

The voice echoed through his mind again.

Su Yang looked at the very dead wolf, savored the rush of power still crackling through his fist, and allowed himself a smile he had not worn in a very long time. "That feeling. Damn, it's good to be back."

Then his gaze drifted to some unseen point in the distance, and killing intent settled over his face like a mask. "Wang Family. Wang Yiyi. Your good days are running out."

...

At that same moment, inside the Wang Manor in Yebei City.

"Patriarch Wang, the job is done. We brothers have taken care of everything."

"After all these years, it's time you honored the promise you made us." The three black-clad figures stood before Wang Lang, who sat enthroned above them.

"Ha ha, of course. You three have done no small amount of work for this patriarch over the years, and I intend to honor my word. However, there is one thing I forgot to mention." Wang Lang lifted the teacup beside him and held the lid in his right hand.

"Oh? What might that be, Patriarch Wang?" The leader of the three frowned.

"Heh heh, nothing really. I simply wanted you three to know — when it comes to taking something from Wang Lang, I may be willing to give it, but you won't live long enough to collect." The instant those words left his mouth, a devastating surge of power erupted from Wang Lang's body.

The three men in black knew exactly what was coming.

Their expressions shifted in unison. Without a word, the leader unleashed his own power and hammered a barrage of fists at Wang Lang: "Wang Lang, you treacherous bastard! You think we came without a backup plan? If the three of us don't walk out of this manor alive today, within the hour every dirty deed your Wang Family has ever committed will be all over Yebei City. The City Lord's Manor will not forgive you."

Wang Lang did not flinch. He just smiled thinly: "Oh? So you suspected I'd silence you all along. Not bad. But even if it gets out, then what?"

"Once my daughter steps into the Divine Martial Realm, what can a mere City Lord's Manor possibly do to the Wang Family?"

"You three know far too much. Best you hurry along to your next lives. And try not to be such fools next time."

"Die."

BOOM.

A blast of spiritual energy erupted and split instantly into three devastating waves, each one hurtling toward a different fleeing figure. The sheer force gave the men in black no room to resist.

Three corpses tumbled from the air in moments.

Utterly lifeless.

"You three served the Wang Family faithfully, I'll grant you that. But you were only outsiders in the end."

"Guards. Drag these bodies out and feed them to the dogs."

Wang Lang set down his teacup, his voice cold as a grave, like a devil wearing a man's face.

Deep within the Wang Manor, a slender, beautiful figure sat in closed-eye meditation, two distinct bloodline auras surging inside her, endlessly merging, endlessly churning.

The power radiating from her was climbing at a staggering pace, higher and higher, with no sign of stopping.
