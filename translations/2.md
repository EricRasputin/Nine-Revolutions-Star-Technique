# Chapter 2: The Mysterious Black Stone

Hearing his daughter say those words, even Wang Lang's mouth twitched.

But he recovered quickly and broke into hearty laughter. "Ha ha ha! My daughter thinks of everything. With this, Su Yang will have absolutely no chance of survival."

"Good. Those who walk the path of cultivation must be ruthless."

"Guards! Sever Su Yang's hand and foot tendons, then throw him into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range. Throw him deep. I want Su Yang — his name, his remains, every trace of his existence — gone from this world forever."

Wang Lang's command rang out into the air. Within moments, several black-clad figures materialized at Su Yang's side.

"Your subordinates hear and obey."

Clean and efficient, the black-clad figures severed Su Yang's tendons, unlocked the iron chains, hoisted him up, and vanished through the stone doorway.

Wang Lang and Wang Yiyi, father and daughter, departed through the stone door as well, hearts brimming with anticipation.

...

Meanwhile, at the outer edge of the Demonic Beast Mountain Range.

The black-clad figures had stopped. None of them showed any intention of going deeper.

"Boss, let's just dump the kid right here. Look at the state of him. If he actually survives this, I, Zhang Kuang, will eat shit standing on my head." One of the black-clad men said.

"He's right, Boss. I hear the Demonic Beast Mountain Range hasn't been safe lately. Apparently some vicious beast has been attacking people left and right. Even Steward Li from the City Lord's Manor nearly lost his life." Another man spoke up, unease plain in his voice.

The one carrying Su Yang heard them. Behind the mask covering his face, his thick brows drew together. He too seemed wary of running into that creature.

After a moment's thought, the leader spoke: "The kid has no blood left in him, and every tendon in his hands and feet has been cut. Even if he was once the Su Family's genius, there is not a chance in hell he survives this. We brothers all served the Su Family once. Let's leave this former prodigy of Yebei City somewhere nearby. Whatever happens, we can at least say we did right by him."

With that, the leader launched himself skyward, stepping off the air itself.

He soared ten meters high, swung his right arm, and hurled Su Yang with everything he had toward the depths of the mountain range.

The force behind it was immense. The body carved a clean arc through the sky.

Only when Su Yang crashed into the dense canopy below and sent birds scattering in every direction did the leader drop back to the ground.

"Let's go. Time to collect our reward from Patriarch Wang."

The words left his lips, and then there was nothing but silence. Not a trace of human presence remained.

Su Yang had been hurled over a thousand meters. By some twist of fate, he landed squarely in a pool of water.

His bloodless body floated on the surface, unmoving.

No different from a corpse.

When the last drop of Supreme Blood left his body, Su Yang knew his fate was essentially sealed.

Without a miracle, this blood debt could only be repaid in the next life.

But in the final fraying thread of his consciousness, he caught Wang Yiyi's words — that she wanted his tendons severed — and something fierce surged up from deep inside him.

*Live. I have to live.*

Sheer willpower kept that last breath locked in his chest as he let the Wang Family's men do with him as they pleased.

He endured it all. Being carried out. Being hauled into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range. Being flung into the pool.

Then those hollow, lightless eyes slowly opened.

"Heh... am I still alive?" Blue sky and white clouds filled his vision. Su Yang blinked, as though trying to bring the world into sharper focus.

Just then, a voice cracked open inside his mind: "You've got one hell of a stubborn life, kid. To survive all of that. The Supreme Bloodline truly does carry a tenacity no ordinary person can match."

Su Yang jolted. His skeletal body trembled. He tried to look around and search for the source, but found his neck would not move at all.

"Save your strength. This Great Sage is inside your body. You need only use your mind to speak with me." The voice came again.

"You... who are you? How are you inside my body?"

"It's a long story. In truth, this Great Sage owes you a debt of gratitude. Do you remember five years ago, when you were ten, you went alone into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range and saved a demonic beast? To repay you for saving its life, the creature gave you its ancestral treasure, a small stone. You thought it looked rather fine, so you kept it on your person."

"This Great Sage is that stone."

Su Yang's mind went blank. Then his thoughts drifted back to the day he had turned ten. He had just broken through to the Tri-Profound Realm, pride fresh in his chest, and ventured alone into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range — not the outer reaches, but deep into the inner region.

His combat prowess back then had been something to fear. After cutting down three fourth-rank demonic beasts in succession, he had stumbled upon a small injured monkey.

The creature looked unbearably frail, slumped against a tree, barely clinging to life.

Su Yang had not thought much of it at the time. He simply saved the monkey. Later, after he left the mountain range and returned to the Su estate, the little creature appeared in his room one night in the dead of hours, bared its teeth and chattered for a long while, then dropped a small stone on the floor and bolted.

All creatures have their own spirit. Su Yang thought nothing more of it. He picked up the stone, found it pleasant to look at — jet-black from end to end, yet impossibly smooth.

And so he had worn it on his person ever since.

Who could have imagined that inside this seemingly ordinary stone, a living being had always been waiting?

*But what difference does that make now? Can I really rely on a stone when I'm like this?*

"So that is how it happened. A pity. I'm already nearly dead. There's no need for you to stay by my side any longer. I hope that one day you can cultivate into human form." Su Yang said, his voice thick with despair.

He truly could not see any path forward. Any way to defy what fate had handed him.

The only things still holding him together were that single breath and that stubborn, half-broken spark of conviction.

"What, ready to give up on your life already? This Great Sage heard those thundering oaths of fury you made just now. Are you really content to let things end like this?" The Great Sage inside the stone spoke again, mockery threading through his voice.

At those words, Su Yang's withered hands curled slowly into fists.

*Was he really going to give up, just like that? Was he really going to let the Wang Family, and that venomous woman, walk free?*

*Was he content to die a discarded blood slave, his body left to rot in the wild until some beast gnawed even his bones to dust?*

No. That was not the ending Su Yang would accept.

He would have his revenge. No matter what.

"Could it be that you have some method, Senior? I've been reduced to this. Without a heaven-defying stroke of fortune, I can only wait for the next life to settle this debt. I refuse to accept it. But I am powerless." Su Yang tightened his fists, drawing on strength he did not know he had left.

"Kid, you really think all the Supreme Blood was drained out of you? Heh heh. Don't forget, this Great Sage has been here all along."

At those words, a spark of hope blazed to life in Su Yang's hollow, lightless eyes.

His body trembling, he stammered: "S-Senior... you mean... my Supreme Blood hasn't been completely drained? Could it be that you..."

"That's right. This Great Sage hasn't been freeloading by your side for nothing all these years. From the moment you put on that stone, I began absorbing your Supreme Blood. At your level of cultivation, you couldn't detect it at all."

"Even during the three years you were imprisoned, I kept absorbing, same as always. Did you really think a mere three years would be enough to drain every drop of Supreme Blood from your body?"

"But I'll say this — that girl is truly vicious. Keeping you penned up like a blood slave. This Great Sage itches to bash her skull in with a staff."
