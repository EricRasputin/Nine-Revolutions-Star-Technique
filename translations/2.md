# Chapter 2: The Mysterious Black Stone

Hearing his daughter utter such vicious words, even Wang Lang's mouth could not help but twitch.

But he quickly broke into hearty laughter: "Ha ha ha, my daughter thinks of everything. With this, Su Yang will have absolutely no chance of survival."

"Indeed — those who walk the path of cultivation must be ruthless and merciless."

"Guards! Sever Su Yang's hand and foot tendons and throw him into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range — throw him deep. I want Su Yang, his name, his very remains — gone from this world forever."

Wang Lang barked the command into the air, and within moments several black-clad figures materialized at Su Yang's side.

"Your subordinates hear and obey."

With that, the black-clad figures severed Su Yang's tendons with swift, clean cuts, unlocked the iron chains, hoisted him up, and vanished through the stone doorway.

Wang Lang and Wang Yiyi, father and daughter, departed through the stone door as well, hearts brimming with anticipation...

...

Meanwhile, at the outer edge of the Demonic Beast Mountain Range.

The black-clad figures had halted in place, showing no intention of venturing any deeper.

"Boss, why don't we just dump the kid right here? Look at the state of him — if he survives this, I, Zhang Kuang, will eat shit standing on my head." One of the black-clad men said.

"He's right, Boss. I hear the Demonic Beast Mountain Range hasn't been safe lately — apparently some ferocious demonic beast has been attacking people left and right. Even Steward Li from the City Lord's Manor nearly lost his life." Another of the men said, a note of fear in his voice.

The one carrying Su Yang heard their words, and beneath the mask covering his face, his thick brows drew together in a tight knot — he too seemed wary of encountering that terrible beast.

After a moment's thought, the leader spoke: "The kid has no blood left in his body, and every tendon in his hands and feet has been cut. Even if he was once the Su Family's genius, there's not a chance in hell he survives. We brothers all served the Su Family once upon a time — let's just leave this former prodigy of Yebei City somewhere nearby. Whatever happens, at least we can say we did right by him."

With that, the leader launched himself skyward, stepping on the very air.

His body soared ten meters high, and with Su Yang dangling from his right hand, he hurled the boy with all his might toward a stretch of the mountain range...

The force was immense, carving a perfect arc through the sky.

Only when Su Yang crashed into the dense canopy below, startling flocks of birds into panicked flight, did the leader drop back to the ground.

"Let's go — time to collect our reward from Patriarch Wang."

The words had barely left his lips before several sharp whooshes split the air, and then there was nothing — not a trace of human presence remained...

Su Yang had been hurled over a thousand meters, and by some twist of fate, he had landed squarely in a pool of water.

His bloodless, fleshless body floated on the surface, utterly still.

No different from a corpse...

When the last drop of Supreme Blood had been drawn from his body, Su Yang knew his fate was all but sealed.

Without a miracle, this blood debt could only be repaid in the next life.

But when the last thread of his fading consciousness caught Wang Yiyi's words — that she wanted his tendons severed — a desire fiercer than anything he had ever known surged through his heart.

'To live — I must live!'

Sheer willpower held that final breath locked in his chest as he let the Wang Family's men do with him as they pleased.

He endured it all — being carried out, hauled into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range, and flung into the pool.

Then, those hollow, lightless eyes slowly opened once more...

"Heh... am I still alive?" Blue sky and white clouds filled his vision. Su Yang blinked, as though trying to bring the world into sharper focus.

Just then, a voice rang out abruptly inside his mind: "You've got one hell of a stubborn life, kid — to survive all of that. The Supreme Bloodline truly does grant a tenacity that ordinary people can't begin to match."

Su Yang jolted in shock, his skeletal frame trembling involuntarily. He tried to look around, searching for whoever had spoken, but found that his neck could no longer move at all.

"Save your strength — this Great Sage is inside your body. You need only use your thoughts to communicate with me." The voice spoke again.

"You... who are you? How are you inside my body?"

"It's a long story. In truth, this Great Sage owes you a debt of gratitude. Do you remember five years ago, when you were ten, you ventured alone into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range and saved a demonic beast? To repay you for saving its life, that creature gave you its ancestral treasure — a small stone. You thought it was rather fine-looking at the time, so you wore it on your person."

"This Great Sage is that very stone."

Su Yang was stunned, and his mind drifted back to the day he had turned ten. He had just broken through to the Tri-Profound Realm and, flush with pride, ventured alone into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range — not merely the outer reaches, but deep into the inner region.

His combat prowess back then had been fearsome indeed — after slaying three fourth-rank demonic beasts in succession, he had stumbled upon an injured little monkey.

The tiny creature looked unbearably frail, slumped against a tree, clinging to the edge of life.

Su Yang had not thought much of it at the time and simply saved the monkey. Later, after he left the mountain range and returned to the Su estate, the little creature appeared in his room one night in the dead of hours — chattering and baring its teeth for a long while before dropping a small stone and scampering off.

All creatures possess their own spirit — Su Yang thought nothing more of it, picked up the stone, and found it rather pleasant to look at: jet-black from end to end, yet impossibly smooth.

And so he had worn that little stone on his person ever since.

Who could have imagined that this seemingly ordinary stone actually harbored a living being?

'But... what difference does it make? Can I really rely on a stone in the state I'm in?'

"So that is how it was... A pity. I'm already on the verge of death. There's no need for you to stay by my side any longer. I hope that one day you can cultivate into human form..." Su Yang said, his voice thick with despair.

He truly could not think of any way to defy fate and reverse his destiny now.

The only things keeping him going were that single breath in his chest and that one stubborn spark of conviction.

"What? Giving up on your life so soon? This Great Sage heard those thundering oaths of fury you made just now — are you really content to let things end like this?" The Great Sage within the stone spoke again, mockery lacing his words.

At those words, Su Yang's withered hands slowly curled into fists.

'Was he really going to give up, just like that? Was he really going to let the Wang Family — and that venomous woman — walk free?'

'Was he willing to die a discarded blood slave, his corpse left to rot in the wild — until some beast or demonic beast found him and gnawed even his bones to dust?'

No! That was not the ending Su Yang wanted.

He would have his revenge — no matter what!

"Could it be that you have some way, Senior? I've been reduced to this... Without a heaven-defying stroke of fortune, I can only wait until the next life to take my revenge. I refuse to accept it... but I am powerless." Su Yang said through clenched fists, drawing on strength he did not know he had.

"Kid, you really think all the Supreme Blood was drained from your body? Heh heh — don't forget, this Great Sage has been here all along."

At those words, a spark of hope blazed to life in Su Yang's hollow, lightless eyes.

His body trembling, he stammered: "S-Senior... are you saying... my Supreme Blood hasn't been completely drained? Could it be that you..."

"That's right. This Great Sage hasn't been freeloading by your side for nothing all these years. From the moment you put on that stone, I began absorbing your Supreme Blood — it's just that at your level of cultivation, you couldn't detect it at all."

"Even during the three years you were imprisoned, this Great Sage kept absorbing as always. Did you really think a mere three years would be enough to drain all the Supreme Blood from your body?"

"But I'll say this — that girl is truly vicious. Penning you up like a blood slave... this Great Sage itches to bash her skull in with a staff!"
