# Chapter 2: The Mysterious Black Stone

Hearing his daughter utter such vicious words, Wang Lang's mouth could not help but twitch.

But he quickly broke into hearty laughter: "Ha ha ha, my daughter thinks of everything. With this, Su Yang will have absolutely no chance of survival."

"Indeed. Those who walk the path of cultivation must be ruthless and merciless."

"Guards! Sever Su Yang's tendons — hands and feet — and throw him into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range. Throw him deep. I want Su Yang — his name, his person, his very remains — to vanish from this world forever."

Wang Lang barked the order into the empty air, and within moments several black-clad figures materialized, converging on Su Yang.

"Your subordinates hear and obey."

With that, the black-clad figures severed Su Yang's tendons with swift, clean cuts, unlocked the iron chains, hoisted him up, and vanished through the stone doorway.

Wang Lang and Wang Yiyi, father and daughter, departed through the stone door as well, their hearts brimming with anticipation...

...

Meanwhile, at the outer edge of the Demonic Beast Mountain Range.

The black-clad figures stood in place, showing no intention of venturing any deeper.

"Boss, why don't we just dump the kid here? Look at the state of him — if he can survive this, I, Zhang Kuang, will eat shit standing on my head."

said one of the men in black.

"He's right, Boss. I've heard the Demonic Beast Mountain Range hasn't been safe lately. Apparently some ferocious demonic beast has been attacking people left and right — even Steward Li from the City Lord's Manor nearly lost his life."

added another, a note of fear in his voice.

The one carrying Su Yang heard their words, and beneath the mask that covered his face, his thick brows drew together in a tight knot — he too seemed wary of encountering that terrible beast.

After a moment of thought, the leader spoke: "The kid has no blood left in his body and every tendon in his hands and feet has been severed. Even if he was once the Su Family's genius, there's not a chance in hell he survives. We brothers all served the Su Family once upon a time — let's just leave this former prodigy of Yebei City somewhere nearby. Whatever happens, at least we'll have given him that much."

With that, the leader launched himself skyward, stepping on the air itself.

His body soared ten meters high, and with Su Yang dangling from his right hand, he hurled the boy with all his might toward a spot deep within the mountain range...

The force was tremendous, carving a perfect arc through the sky.

Only when Su Yang crashed into the dense canopy below, startling flocks of birds into panicked flight, did the leader drop back to the ground.

"Let's go. Time to collect our reward from Patriarch Wang."

The words had barely left his lips before several whooshes split the air, and then there was nothing — not a trace of human presence remained...

Su Yang had been hurled over a thousand meters, and by some twist of fate, he had landed squarely in a pool of water.

His bloodless, wasted body floated on the surface, utterly still.

No different from a corpse...

When the last drop of Supreme Blood had been drawn from his body, Su Yang knew that his fate was all but sealed.

Without a miracle, this blood debt could only be repaid in the next life.

But when the last thread of his fading consciousness caught Wang Yiyi's words — that she wanted to sever his tendons — a desire fiercer than anything he had ever known surged through his heart.

'To live. He had to live!'

Sheer willpower held that final breath locked inside his chest as he let the Wang Family's men do with him as they pleased.

He endured it all — being carried out, hauled into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range, and hurled into the pool.

Then, those hollow, lightless eyes slowly opened once more...

"Heh... am I still alive?" Blue sky and white clouds filled his vision. Su Yang blinked, as though trying to bring the world into sharper focus.

Just as the thought crossed his mind, a voice rang out inside his head without warning: "You've got one hell of a stubborn life, kid. To survive all that — the Supreme Bloodline really does grant a tenacity that ordinary people can't begin to match."

Su Yang jolted in shock, his skeletal frame trembling involuntarily. He tried to look around, searching for whoever had spoken, but found that his neck could no longer move at all.

"Save your strength. This Great Sage is inside your body. You need only communicate with me through your thoughts..." the voice said again.

"You... who are you? How are you inside my body?"

"It is a long story. In truth, this Great Sage owes you a debt of gratitude. Do you remember, five years ago when you were ten, you ventured alone into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range and saved a demonic beast? To repay you for saving its life, that beast gave you its ancestral treasure — a small stone. You thought it was rather fine-looking at the time, so you wore it on your person."

"And this Great Sage is that very stone."

Su Yang was stunned, and his mind drifted back to the day he had just turned ten. He had recently broken through to the Tri-Profound Realm, and flush with youthful pride, he had ventured alone into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range — not just the outer reaches, but deep into the inner region.

His combat prowess back then had been fearsome indeed. After slaying three fourth-rank demonic beasts in succession, he stumbled upon an injured little monkey.

The tiny creature looked unbearably frail, slumped against a tree, clinging to the edge of life.

Su Yang had not thought much of it at the time and simply saved the little monkey. Later, after he left the mountain range and returned to the Su estate, the monkey appeared one night in the dead of hours, clutching a small stone in its paws. It chattered and bared its teeth for a good while, then dropped the stone and scampered off.

All creatures possess their own spirit. Su Yang thought nothing more of it, picked up the little stone, and found it rather handsome — jet-black from end to end, yet impossibly smooth.

And so he had worn that little stone on his person ever since.

Who could have imagined that this seemingly ordinary little stone actually harbored a living being?

'And yet... what difference did it make? In his current state, could he really rely on a little stone?'

"So that is how it was... A pity. I am already on the verge of death. There is no need for you to stay with me any longer. I hope that one day, you can cultivate into human form..."

Su Yang said, his voice thick with despair.

He truly could not think of any way to defy fate and reverse his destiny now.

The only thing keeping him going was that single breath in his chest, that one stubborn spark of conviction.

"What? Giving up on your life so soon? This Great Sage heard those thunderous oaths of fury you swore just now. Are you really content to let things end like this?" The Great Sage within the stone spoke again, a hint of mockery lacing his words.

At those words, Su Yang's withered hands slowly clenched into fists.

'Was he really going to give up, just like this? Was he really going to let the Wang Family — and that venomous woman — walk free?'

'Was he willing to be a discarded blood slave, his corpse left to rot in the wilderness, until wild beasts or demonic beasts found him and gnawed even his bones to dust?'

No! That was not the ending Su Yang wanted.

He would have his revenge. He would have it no matter what!

"Could it be that you have some way, Senior? I have been reduced to this... Without a heaven-defying stroke of fortune, I can only wait until the next life to take my revenge. I refuse to accept it... but I am powerless."

Su Yang said through clenched fists, drawing on strength he did not know he had.

"Kid, you really think your Supreme Blood was completely drained? Heh heh — don't forget, this Great Sage has been here all along."

At those words, a spark of hope blazed to life in Su Yang's hollow, lightless eyes.

His entire body trembling, he stammered: "S-Senior... are you saying... my Supreme Blood was not completely drained? Could it be that you..."

"That's right. This Great Sage hasn't been freeloading at your side for nothing these past few years. From the very moment you put on that stone, I began absorbing your Supreme Blood. It's just that with your level of cultivation, you couldn't detect it at all."

"Even during the three years of your imprisonment, this Great Sage continued absorbing as always. Did you really think a mere three years would be enough to drain all the Supreme Blood from your body?"

"But I'll say this much — that girl is truly vicious. Penning you up like a blood slave... This Great Sage wants to bash her skull in with a staff!"
