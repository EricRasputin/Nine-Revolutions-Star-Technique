# Chapter 1: Three Years as a Blood Slave

_The Eastern Xuan Continent.
_
_Within Yebei City!
_
Inside the Wang Manor's secret chamber, a woman was opening a stone door — it groaned with a heavy click, then slowly ground aside.

The sight that greeted her was an emaciated young man, shackled behind the door by several black iron chains that held him fast.

The youth's face was deathly pale, drained of every trace of color, and only the faintest whisper of breathing proved he was still alive.

The woman approached the youth with light, unhurried steps, her figure slender and lovely.

"Su Yang, today is the last time I'll be drawing your blood. I must say, you held out longer than I expected — it took a full three years to drain every last drop of Supreme Blood from your body. You really have suffered." The woman gazed at the frail youth before her, her tone ice-cold.

"Heh heh... heh heh!"

"Wang... Yiyi, even if you drain every drop of Supreme Blood from my body... what of it? That bloodline will never truly be yours. I, Su Yang, swear I will never let you go — not even in death!" The youth's name was Su Yang, formerly the Young Master of the Su Family of Yebei City, once the pride of his clan and the most renowned prodigy the city had ever produced. Born with the Supreme Bloodline, he had stepped into the Spirit Transformation Realm at three, reached the True Martial Realm by five, and entered the Tri-Profound Realm at the age of ten.

With such a dazzling record, everyone had believed Su Yang was destined to become the first cultivator in Yebei City's history to break through to the Divine Martial Realm before the age of fifteen.

Yet a single marriage had caused Su Yang to vanish from the world entirely.

Yebei City had long been governed by three great powers: the City Lord's Manor, the Su Family, and the Wang Family. The three factions had coexisted in peace — until Su Yang was born, and the emergence of the Supreme Bloodline drove both the City Lord's Manor and the Wang Family to seek a marriage alliance with the Su clan.

When Su Yang turned twelve, the three families gathered together and let him choose his future bride, with the engagement to be formalized at once and the wedding held after he turned fifteen.

No one could say which eye Su Yang had been blind in when he chose the Wang Family's daughter — the very woman standing in this secret chamber now — Wang Yiyi.

It was only after the Wang Family conspired to trap him, binding him with eight Soul-Suppressing Iron Chains and sealing him inside this chamber, that Su Yang finally understood: the so-called marriage alliance had been nothing but a grand conspiracy from the start.

The Supreme Bloodline was rare beyond measure. Wang Yiyi herself carried the Fire Phoenix Bloodline, and if it could be fused with the Supreme Bloodline until the two merged as one, the Wang Family would inevitably produce a supreme powerhouse — at which point neither the City Lord's Manor nor the Su Family would be worth fearing.

And so, for three long years, Wang Yiyi had visited the chamber once every three days to collect a vial of Su Yang's Supreme Blood.

Three years — Su Yang could no longer remember how he had survived them.

As for the Su Family, Su Yang's disappearance had plunged them into internal strife — his parents were framed by the Grand Elder, who had long coveted the position of family head, and exiled them to the frontier. Everyone connected to Su Yang was reassigned or driven out, and those who refused to submit were struck down publicly by the Grand Elder as a warning to all.

From that day forward, the Su Family fell into utter ruin, controlled by the Grand Elder and reduced to a puppet clan of the Wang Family.

"Su Yang, rest easy. I, Wang Yiyi, am no ungrateful wretch. All these years, I have you to thank — you and that bloodline of yours. Thanks to you, I've reached Perfection in the Tri-Profound Realm in a single leap, and after I draw your blood one more time today, I'll be ready to assault the Divine Martial Realm."

"When the time comes, I will be sure to repay you properly."

"Hmph!"

Even as she spoke, the woman produced a gleaming little knife and plunged it ruthlessly into Su Yang's skin — skin so ravaged there was scarcely any flesh left to cut.

Golden blood began to seep out, drop by agonizing drop, impossibly slow...

She pulled out a jade vial and pressed it hastily beneath the wound. As the golden blood dripped into the vial, excitement crept unbidden across her face.

Just one more step — just this one small vial. One final absorption of Supreme Blood, and she would become the most dazzling prodigy Yebei City had ever seen!

Su Yang could no longer feel the pain — that sensation had long since become routine, and there was not a shred of strength left in his entire body.

His dantian had long since run dry, devoid of even a wisp of spiritual energy... Within his Eight Extraordinary Meridians, only the barest trickle of blood still flowed...

It was hard to imagine that in a body so ravaged, Su Yang still had the strength to speak at all.

Watching the greed plastered across Wang Yiyi's face, Su Yang could not help but laugh: "Heh... heh heh, ha ha ha!" The laughter was faint, yet it carried with perfect clarity to Wang Yiyi's ears.

Wang Yiyi frowned, unable to fathom why a man on death's very doorstep would burst into laughter.

_'Has he lost his mind?'_

"Wang Yiyi, you truly are ruthless. To think that I, Su Yang — prodigy of the Su Family — would be taken in by your honeyed words. You didn't just deceive me. You stole my Supreme Blood and penned me up like a blood slave."

"You! What a vicious heart."

Su Yang lifted his gaze — and in those hollow, lightless pupils, a surge of killing intent fiercer than anything he had ever felt erupted without warning, locking onto Wang Yiyi with lethal intensity.

Wang Yiyi, who had been extracting his blood with painstaking care, recoiled at the force of his stare — her delicate hand trembled, and a single drop of Supreme Blood fell to the ground...

She had never expected Su Yang to still possess such a terrifying, murderous gaze, and a chill of fear rose unbidden in her chest. But the feeling vanished just as quickly — what a joke; he was already a cripple, and once she finished draining this last batch of blood, he would be dead. Why should she fear a dead man?

Still, that wasted drop of Supreme Blood left Wang Yiyi seething — her already frigid expression turned glacial as she spoke: "Su Yang, spare me that look. The path of cultivation has always been cruel — you simply got what you deserved. Who told you to trust me so blindly? Truly laughable."

"Heh heh, yes. Truly laughable indeed."

"Laughable that I was blind. Laughable that I never saw through your Wang Family's scheme sooner. Laughable that I actually believed your words and let myself be trapped so easily."

"Wang Yiyi, mark my words. If I, Su Yang, do not die today, you had best sleep with one eye open because I will bring ruin upon your entire Wang Family and cut down every last one of you!" Su Yang glared at the venomous woman before him, biting off each word through clenched teeth, the fury in his eyes blazing hot enough to ignite twin infernos and burn her to ash.

"Enough with the empty threats. Save your breath. Rest assured, I won't let you die easily. Even after every drop of Supreme Blood has been drained from your body, I will see to it that you end this life in the most agonizing way imaginable."

Wang Yiyi's tone remained ice-cold. Su Yang said nothing more, only lowering his head in silence as the furious light in his eyes faded, replaced once more by hollow emptiness...

"Hm? There isn't a single drop of Supreme Blood left?" Wang Yiyi stared at the wound — not another drop of golden blood emerged — and frowned.

She shook the jade vial in her hand, and her brow furrowed deeper still.

She glanced at Su Yang's bowed head, then snatched up the knife again and stabbed him several more times in quick succession.

Su Yang did not stir — as though he were already dead.

Not a single drop of Supreme Blood emerged from the fresh wounds.

Wang Yiyi surveyed the scene, her frost-cold expression turning even icier: "How infuriating — only half a vial. And I wasted a drop because of you, worthless wretch!"

Whether Su Yang heard her or not was unclear — Wang Yiyi lifted his skull-like face with one hand, seeming to want to say something more, but his tightly shut eyes made her swallow the words, and she simply released him and rose to her feet.

At that moment, another figure stepped through the stone doorway.

"Yiyi, how did it go? Was this final extraction a success?" The newcomer's voice boomed — he appeared to be in his forties or fifties, with a square jaw, a hooked nose, and eyes that flickered with barely perceptible cunning.

"Not entirely, Father. There isn't a single drop of Supreme Blood left in Su Yang's body. This time, I only managed to collect half a vial." Wang Yiyi offered a slight bow upon seeing the arrival.

"Oh? Only half a vial? What a pity." The man furrowed his brow, genuine regret coloring his tone.

This was none other than the Wang Family's patriarch — Wang Lang — one of Yebei City's three greatest experts, a cultivator with a Divine Martial Realm cultivation base.

"No matter. By now, your Fire Phoenix Bloodline and the Supreme Bloodline have nearly finished merging. Even half a vial should be enough for you to break through to the Divine Martial Realm. Come — it's time to put an end to all of this." Wang Lang regarded his daughter, his voice calm and measured.

But behind his eyes, visions of a grand future flickered and gleamed.

As long as his daughter could forge the Supreme Fire Phoenix Bloodline and step into the Divine Martial Realm, the Wang Family would one day claim a seat of power across the entire Eastern Xuan Continent!

"Mm, it shouldn't be a problem."

"As for Su Yang..." Wang Yiyi began, seeming to seek her father's judgment.

"The Su Family already believes he's dead. He's nothing but a cripple now anyway, and it wouldn't do to let him die on our doorstep. After all, his father and I were once on good terms. He was betrothed to you, too. Without his sacrifice, you wouldn't be who you are today."

"I'll have someone toss him into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range shortly." Wang Lang cast a final glance at Su Yang, still bound in iron chains, the corner of his mouth curling upward.

"I leave everything to Father's judgment. However, your daughter has one more request."

"Oh? What would that be?"

Wang Yiyi turned around and glanced once more at Su Yang, half-kneeling on the ground. Remembering the terrifying look he had fixed on her moments ago, she turned away and spoke in a ghostly murmur: "I want to sever the tendons in his hands and feet..."
