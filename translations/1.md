# Chapter 1: Three Years as a Blood Slave

The Eastern Xuan Continent.

Yebei City.

A stone door groaned open inside the Wang Manor's secret chamber, the iron mechanism clicking as the heavy slab swung slowly aside.

Behind it was an emaciated young man, held fast by several black iron chains bolted into the wall.

The youth's face was deathly pale. Not a trace of color touched his skin, and only the faintest thread of breath proved he was still alive.

The woman moved toward him with light, unhurried steps, her figure slender and graceful.

"Su Yang, today is the last time I'll be drawing your blood. I have to say, you held out longer than I expected. Three full years to drain every drop of Supreme Blood from your body. You've truly suffered." The woman's gaze settled on the wasted youth before her, her tone as cold as stone.

"Heh heh... heh heh!"

"Wang... Yiyi. Even if you drain every last drop of Supreme Blood from my body, what of it? That bloodline will never truly be yours. I, Su Yang, swear I will not let you go. Not even in death!" The youth's name was Su Yang, formerly the Young Master of the Su Family of Yebei City, once the clan's pride and the city's most celebrated prodigy. Born with the Supreme Bloodline, he had stepped into the Spirit Transformation Realm at three, reached the True Martial Realm by five, and at ten, entered the Tri-Profound Realm.

With a record like that, all of Yebei City had believed Su Yang would become its first cultivator to breach the Divine Martial Realm before the age of fifteen.

But a single betrothal had erased him from the world entirely.

Yebei City had long been held in balance by three great powers: the City Lord's Manor, the Su Family, and the Wang Family. The three factions coexisted in peace until Su Yang was born. The emergence of the Supreme Bloodline sent both the City Lord's Manor and the Wang Family scrambling to forge a marriage alliance with the Su clan.

When Su Yang turned twelve, the three families sat down together and let him choose his future bride. The engagement was to be formalized at once, the wedding to follow after his fifteenth birthday.

No one could say which eye Su Yang had been blind in when he chose the Wang Family's daughter — the very woman now standing in this chamber — Wang Yiyi.

It was only after the Wang Family conspired to capture him, binding him with eight Soul-Suppressing Iron Chains and sealing him inside this room, that Su Yang finally understood: the so-called marriage alliance had been nothing but a grand trap from the very beginning.

The Supreme Bloodline was rare beyond measure. Wang Yiyi herself carried the Fire Phoenix Bloodline, and if the two could be fused until they merged as one, the Wang Family would inevitably produce a supreme powerhouse. Neither the City Lord's Manor nor the Su Family would be worth fearing after that.

And so, for three years, Wang Yiyi had come to the chamber once every three days to collect a vial of Su Yang's Supreme Blood.

Three years. Su Yang could no longer remember how he had survived them.

As for the Su Family, Su Yang's disappearance had plunged them into internal strife. His parents were framed by the Grand Elder, who had long coveted the position of family head, and sent into exile. Everyone connected to Su Yang was reassigned or driven out, and those who refused to comply were struck down publicly by the Grand Elder as a warning to the rest.

From that day on, the Su Family fell into ruin, seized by the Grand Elder and reduced to a puppet clan of the Wang Family.

"Su Yang, put your mind at ease. I, Wang Yiyi, am no ungrateful wretch. These years, I have you to thank — you and that bloodline of yours. You carried me all the way to Perfection in the Tri-Profound Realm in one breath, and after this final drawing today, I will be ready to assault the Divine Martial Realm."

"When the time comes, I will repay you properly."

"Hmph!"

Even as she spoke, the woman produced a gleaming little knife and drove it without mercy into Su Yang's skin, skin so ravaged there was scarcely flesh left beneath it.

Golden blood began to seep out, drop by drop, achingly slow.

She drew out a jade vial and pressed it beneath the wound. As the golden drops fell into the vial, excitement crept unbidden across her face.

Just one more step. Just this one small vial. One final absorption of Supreme Blood, and she would be the most dazzling prodigy Yebei City had ever produced.

Su Yang could no longer feel pain. The sensation had long since become routine, and there was not a shred of strength left anywhere in his body.

His dantian had run dry long ago, empty of even a wisp of spiritual energy. Within his Eight Extraordinary Meridians, only the barest trickle of blood still moved.

It was hard to imagine that a body so ravaged could still find the strength to speak at all.

Watching the greed spread across Wang Yiyi's face, Su Yang let out a laugh. "Heh... heh heh, ha ha ha!" It was barely a sound, yet it carried with perfect clarity to Wang Yiyi's ears.

Wang Yiyi frowned. She could not fathom why a man on death's doorstep would burst into laughter.

*Has he lost his mind?*

"Wang Yiyi, you truly are ruthless. To think that I, Su Yang, prodigy of the Su Family, would be taken in by your honeyed words. You didn't just deceive me. You stole my Supreme Blood and kept me penned up like a blood slave."

"You. What a vicious heart."

Su Yang raised his gaze, and in those hollow, lightless pupils, a surge of killing intent fiercer than anything he had ever summoned erupted without warning, locking onto Wang Yiyi with absolute ferocity.

Wang Yiyi had been collecting his blood with careful hands. The force of his stare made her fingers tremble, and a single drop of Supreme Blood slipped from the vial and fell to the floor.

She had not expected Su Yang to still carry such a killing edge. A cold knot of fear tightened in her chest before she dismissed it. He was a cripple. The moment she finished this last batch, he would be dead. There was nothing to fear from a dead man.

Still, that wasted drop left Wang Yiyi furious. Her already frigid expression dropped another degree: "Su Yang, spare me that look. The path of cultivation has always been cruel. You simply got what you deserved. Who told you to trust me so easily? Truly laughable."

"Heh heh, yes. Truly laughable."

"Laughable that I was blind. Laughable that I never saw through your Wang Family's scheme sooner. Laughable that I believed your words and let myself be trapped so easily."

"Wang Yiyi, mark my words. If I, Su Yang, do not die today, you had best sleep with one eye open. I will bring ruin upon your entire Wang Family and cut down every last one of you." Su Yang fixed his gaze on the woman before him and bit off each word through clenched teeth, the fury in his eyes blazing hot enough to scorch the air between them.

"Enough with the empty words. Save your breath. Rest assured, I won't let you die easily. Even after every last drop of Supreme Blood has been drained from your body, I will make certain you end this life in the most agonizing way possible."

Wang Yiyi's tone did not change. Su Yang said nothing more. He simply lowered his head in silence, the furious light in his eyes guttering out, replaced once again by hollow emptiness.

"Hm? Not a single drop of Supreme Blood left?" Wang Yiyi stared at the wound. Nothing came out. She frowned.

She shook the jade vial, and her brow furrowed deeper still.

She glanced at Su Yang's bowed head, then snatched up the knife and drove it into his body several more times in quick succession.

Su Yang did not stir. He might have already been dead.

Not one drop of Supreme Blood emerged from the fresh wounds.

Wang Yiyi surveyed the scene, her frost-cold expression sharpening into something colder still: "Infuriating. Only half a vial, and I wasted a drop because of you. Worthless wretch."

Whether Su Yang heard her or not was impossible to tell. Wang Yiyi lifted his skull-like face with one hand, seeming to want to say something more, then thought better of it. She let him go and rose to her feet.

At that moment, another figure stepped through the stone doorway.

"Yiyi, how did it go? Was the final extraction a success?" The newcomer's voice was loud and carried easily. He appeared to be in his forties or fifties, square-jawed, hawk-nosed, with eyes that harbored a cunning one could easily miss.

"Not entirely, Father. Su Yang's body has nothing left in it. This time I only managed to collect half a vial." Wang Yiyi offered a slight bow as the man entered.

"Oh? Only half a vial. What a pity." He pressed his brows together, genuine regret in his voice.

This was Wang Lang, patriarch of the Wang Family, one of Yebei City's three greatest experts, a cultivator standing in the Divine Martial Realm.

"No matter. Your Fire Phoenix Bloodline and the Supreme Bloodline have nearly finished merging. Half a vial should still be enough to carry you through to the Divine Martial Realm. Come. It's time to close the chapter on all of this." Wang Lang regarded his daughter, his voice calm and measured.

The light behind his eyes told a different story.

Once his daughter forged the Supreme Fire Phoenix Bloodline and stepped into the Divine Martial Realm, the Wang Family would carve out a seat of power across the entire Eastern Xuan Continent.

"Mm. It shouldn't be a problem."

"As for Su Yang..." Wang Yiyi began, as if seeking her father's decision.

"The Su Family already believes he's dead. He's a cripple regardless, and it wouldn't look right to let him die on our grounds. His father and I were on good terms once. He was betrothed to you. Without his sacrifice, you wouldn't be where you are today."

"I'll have someone throw him into the Demonic Beast Mountain Range." Wang Lang cast a final glance at Su Yang, still shackled in his chains, and the corner of his mouth lifted slightly.

"I defer to Father's judgment entirely. But your daughter has one more request."

"Oh? What is it?"

Wang Yiyi turned and looked once more at Su Yang, half-collapsed on the ground. She thought of the look he had fixed on her moments ago. Then she turned away, her voice going quiet: "I want to sever the tendons in his hands and feet."
