# Chapter 4: The Battle Sage Art!

In a stone cave deep within the Demonic Beast Mountain Range.

Su Yang sat cross-legged, using his divine sense to communicate with the stone about something.

"Senior Great Sage, are you saying you want me to come inside the stone?" Su Yang asked, staring at the black stone hanging from his chest in bewilderment.

"That's right. Don't go thinking this is just some ordinary rock. Send your divine sense in, and I guarantee you'll be in for a shock." The stone replied.

"Alright, I'll send my divine sense in now."

Without further hesitation, Su Yang extended his divine sense toward the black stone.

The instant his divine sense touched the black stone, a tremendous suction force surged forth. Before Su Yang could even react, his entire body vanished from the cave. All that remained was a black stone, lying quietly on the ground...

As the dizziness gradually faded, Su Yang slowly opened his eyes.

Before him stretched an endless expanse of stars — a sky full of celestial bodies and meteorites of every size.

Su Yang was dumbstruck, his jaw working soundlessly, his eyes blown wide. Never in his wildest dreams had he imagined that this tiny stone contained an entire universe — an entire starfield.

"Th-this place is..."

Su Yang stammered, struggling to form the question.

"Not a bad-looking kid, I'll say. Over here — this Great Sage is right over here." The familiar voice rang out again, and Su Yang turned to look in the direction it came from.

Not far away, atop a conspicuously massive meteorite, he could make out what appeared to be a figure.

Su Yang stared wide-eyed and called out, "Senior Great Sage, is that you?"

"Hmph! Who else would it be? In this godforsaken place, there's no one here but me." The voice carried a hint of irritation.

Su Yang scrambled toward the massive meteorite without a moment's delay.

Before long, he stood atop the colossal rock — and the scene before him left him stunned all over again.

A humanoid stone statue stood at the dead center of the meteorite.

And above it, an enormous stone palm pressed down, pinning it in place.

Su Yang had truly never seen anything this bizarre...

"Stop gawking. The statue you're looking at — that's this Great Sage." The voice spoke again, and Su Yang hurried to the statue's side, studying it closely for a long moment.

He noticed the statue did not look entirely human — the face, at least, had a pointed chin and sunken cheeks... like a monkey's.

"You're... Senior Great Sage??" Su Yang reached out to poke the statue, wanting to verify whether it was real or fake, but before his finger even made contact, a violent force erupted from the statue's surface, instantly blasting Su Yang back several zhang. Blood trickled from the corner of his mouth.

"Touch me again without permission, kid, and you're dead."

"This is no ordinary place. See that stone palm overhead? If that thing unleashes its power, it'll erase you in an instant."

Su Yang shuddered involuntarily, grateful he had not touched anything else...

"Kid, don't ask too many questions. What you need to know, this Great Sage will tell you in time. Right now, you should be worrying about yourself. You were born with the Supreme Bloodline — your future should have been limitless. Three years ago you had already reached the Tri-Profound Realm, and now you're nothing but a cripple. Your dantian doesn't hold a shred of spiritual energy. Vengeance is not something you'll achieve overnight."

"That girl carries the Fire Phoenix Bloodline. If she truly manages to fuse it with your Supreme Bloodline, she'll become a terrifying existence."

"Your path to revenge will only grow harder."

The Great Sage's voice echoed once more.

Su Yang took the words to heart. He knew how weak he was right now, and that this was no time to pry into matters beyond his concern. He turned toward the statue and bowed respectfully. "If the Senior Great Sage summoned this junior here, then surely you must have a way to defy fate and change my destiny."

"I beg you, Senior — if there is even a sliver of hope, this junior is willing to try."

"Hmm, not a bad resolve. But recovering to the Tri-Profound Realm in a short time is essentially impossible. As you can see, this Great Sage is nothing more than a stone statue — there's not much I can do for you."

"However, this Great Sage does have one method that can boost your combat strength in a short time — without needing a drop of spiritual energy. The catch is this: in a thousand years, no one besides this Great Sage has ever been able to complete it. And once you begin training in this method, you must see it through to the end. Abandon it halfway, and you will be reduced to a cripple beyond saving."

"Do you dare try?"

Su Yang listened to every word in his mind with absolute focus.

The moment the voice fell silent, Su Yang answered without hesitation. "This junior is willing."

"Ha ha ha! Kid, you'd better think this through. Even a thousand years ago, this method of mine struck terror into all who heard of it. Even with the Supreme Bloodline, you may not be able to endure it."

"This Great Sage will ask you one more time — do you dare try?"

Su Yang straightened his back and took a step toward the statue. "What is there to fear? Look at me — even with the Supreme Bloodline, returning to the peak is a distant dream. As you said, this junior's path to vengeance will be anything but easy. If it means growing stronger, no matter how bitter, no matter how grueling, even if I must walk a razor's edge — this junior is willing. No regrets."

After Su Yang finished speaking, the statue fell silent.

After a pause, a booming laugh erupted from the stone. "Ha ha ha! Good! Not bad at all — you've got the same fire this Great Sage had in his youth."

"Very well. Then I'll let you give this method a try."

"Come to my side."

Su Yang did not hesitate and walked straight to the statue.

"Kneel!"

Without a moment's pause, Su Yang dropped to his knees with a thud.

"I'll spare you the kowtow. From this day forward, you are this Great Sage's disciple. Today, I shall pass on to you my combat art — the Battle Sage Art. To cultivate this technique requires no spiritual energy. You need only your body, your willpower, and your flesh and blood — fighting ceaselessly to forge your strength."

"Now, this Great Sage will engrave the cultivation method of the Battle Sage Art directly into your mind. Starting today, you will train here for twenty hours each day — you may not leave before those twenty hours are up. Can you do it?"

Su Yang felt his skull splitting with pain as image after image flooded into his mind like a rushing spring.

Cold sweat poured from his body, and his teeth ground together so hard they creaked.

At last, bracing himself on the ground with both hands and gasping for breath, he managed a reply: "Your disciple... will see it done!"

"Good. Rest up — we begin shortly."

"The Battle Sage Art holds no rank or grade — it is a combat method created solely by this Great Sage. Only with my direct guidance can you learn it in the shortest time possible."

"If you can endure it, within four months, this Great Sage will grant you combat power rivaling the Divine Martial Realm."

A spark of light flashed in Su Yang's eyes — joy and hunger, burning bright.

"Senior Great Sage, I'm ready."

"Please — start now... and don't hold back!"
